The Death of the Animated GIF (And Why We Must Fight Back)

Filed under: Webmaster Rants | Posted on: May 30, 1996

They say the GIF format is dead. They say Unisys patents will strangle the free web. But we say no! Without the animated blinking mailbox, the spinning under construction signs, and the rotating glowing email icons, the web will become a quiet, corporate, lifeless wasteland of static grids.

As builders of cyberspace, we have an obligation to safeguard these animations. Support the LZW-free initiatives, but do not dump your badges!

Why Flat Design is a Corporate Conspiracy

Filed under: Cyber Conspiracy | Posted on: March 1, 1996

Mark my words: the disappearance of 3D beveled borders is not a stylistic evolution; it is a corporate scheme to minimize graphics caching. They want to train your eyes to accept low-detail layouts so you buy low-bandwidth subscription services!

By stripping away the highlights and shadows, they make the web feel flat, un-interactive, and corporate. Resist the flat takeover! Keep your panels beveled.
